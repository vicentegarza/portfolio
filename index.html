WELCOME!

I am from Monterrey, Mexico. I am a retired professor of Chemical Engineering. I was a full-time professor at Tec de Monterrey for 25 years where I taught several courses including:

  • Transport Phenomena 💡
  • Momentum Transfer Ops. (Fluid Mechanics) 💡
  • Chemical Reactor Engineering 💡
  • Heat Transfer Ops. 💡
  • Fundamentals of Microprocesses 💡

Students learn how to create mathematical models to predict: fluid flow (momentum transfer), temperature distribution in solids (heat transfer), species concentration distributions (mass transfer).

Students learn how to analyze and design piping systems for both liquid and gas flow, specify pumps and compressors, select and design flow meters, etc.

Students learn how to analyze and design chemical reactors and understand how the operating conditions such as temperature and pressure affect performance.

Students learn how to analyze and design heat exchangers such as: double-pipe HX, tube-and-shell HX and plate HX. They also learn how to design other heat transfer equipment such as evaporators.

Students learn about the advantanges and disadvantages of micro-processes and they learn how to model and design mixers, heat transfer equipment and microreactors.
